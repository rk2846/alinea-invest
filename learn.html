The Bible

What is Blockchain?

Blockchain is the core technology underlying Bitcoin and other digital currencies, however, they are separate entities. Blockchain technology is immutable, decentralized, and transparent. It can simply be explained as a distributed public ledger which records all electronic transactions (data) that happen between two parties as chronological “blocks” on a network (like a shared google doc) maintained by multiple nodes (computers). The ledger allows for information to be recorded and distributed rather than copied. The first block on the chain is called the “genesis” block.

You can learn more about blockchain here

What are cryptocurrencies?

Cryptocurrencies are a medium of exchange, a store of value, and a unit of account. They are an investment for the future. Cryptocurrencies are created through an ICO (initial coin offering), which is crowdfunding via the use of cryptocurrencies to issue a token like Bitcoin or Ethereum.

Sally Davies, a FT Technology Reporter, famously said “[Blockchain] is to Bitcoin, what the internet is to email. A big electronic system, on top of which you can build applications. Currency is just one”.

Much like DNA in every cell of an organism, the ledger is effectively the DNA of the cryptocurrency. The currency, then, can never be subjected to any central authority, it can never be interfered with or manipulated.

Cryptocurrency is a new form of money



Glossary

Address: refers to the place where cryptocurrency can be sent to & from (in the form of a series of letters + numbers)
Altcoin: alternative coins ~ usually any cryptocurrency other than Bitcoin.
Arbitrage: a strategy ~ taking advantage of differences in price of the same commodity in two or more markets or exchanges (ex: cryptocurrency prices on Korean exchanges can be different from those on US exchanges. An arbitrage trader would buy in one and sell in another for profit)
Bear: a trader that is pessimistic about market prices; ‘bearish’ about the market/price and expects them to go down
Bitcoin ATM: also known as BTM ~ a machine you can withdraw Bitcoin from.
Bits: sub unit of 1 bitcoin. There are 1,000,000 bits in 1 bitcoin.
Block: collection of transactions data happening every time period on a blockchain. For bitcoin a block is created every 10 minutes.
Bubble: occurs when market participants drive prices up above their value and is usually followed by a steep + rapid drop in prices.
Bull: a trader that is optimistic about market prices; ‘bullish’ about the market/price and expects them to go up
Cold Storage: offline storage to keep cryptocurrencies (wallet not connected to the internet)
Consensus: occurs when all nodes in the network agree on which block to produce next.
Cryptocurrency: a digital/virtual currency operating independently from a central authority. “Crypto” comes from the use of cryptography, used to solve several core problems in transferring money over the internet, eliminating the unauthorized creation of new money (referred to as “double-spend”).
Dapp: Decentralized Application. An application that is built using Ethereum smart contract code
Decentralization: a network that is not managed by any central power or authority
ETF: Exchange Traded Fund. A group of assets such as stocks, bonds, and cryptocurrencies that can be traded (bought or sold) like a single asset.
Exchange: online platforms where people can buy/sell cryptocurrencies
Fork: occurs when developers in the network decide to change the code/software of the platform ~ splits the blockchain into 2 branches/chains
FIAT: Government issued currency (for example - the US dollar or UK pound)
HODL: Originally a typo on a cryptocurrency forum, when someone typed HODL instead of HOLD. Interpreted as “Hold On for Dear Life”, it’s become the battle cry of diehard cryptocurrency investors who believe that the investment will come good one day. *crypto meme
ICO: Initial Coin Offering. A type of crowdfunding using cryptocurrencies to raise capital (money) for early-stage companies. Recently has come under fire due to scams & market manipulations
KYC: Know Your Customer. Obligatory process to verify the identity of a customer (in regards to global anti-money laundering laws)
Lambo: short for Lamborghinis. What we are all going to buy when we’re rich. *crypto meme
Long: a trade situation where you buy a cryptocurrency with the expectation of selling it at a higher price for profit later.
Margin call: occurs when the value of your collateral decreases outside of a safe range
Miners: everyday people because anyone with the right tool can mine crypto and earn tokens in return, the mining is done by computers however.
Mining: the process of performing expensive computer calculations to create and verify groups of transactions (blocks) on a blockchain
Mooning: when the price goes up to very high levels. *meme
Node: a server or computer in the network that holds a copy of the blockchain; nodes are run by miners and validate & relay transactions while receiving a copy of the full blockchain to ensure immutability.
OTC: Over The Counter. Transactions made outside of an exchange.
PoS: Proof-of-Stake. Has “validators” instead of miners that forge new blocks instead of mining them. To become a validator, one must send a certain amount of coins to the network as stake, which is similar to a security deposit. So instead of having miners solve a problem and be rewarded, the Proof-of-Stake method chooses the creator of a new block in a decisive way, depending on one’s stake (wealth) and there is no block reward. The main advantages that the PoS method offers is that it minimizes electricity costs (more cost effective) and offers more fairness in its reward (gives people more of a chance).
PoW: Proof-of-Work. Blockchain consensus mechanism which solves computationally intensive puzzles to validate transactions and create new blocks using miners. Bitcoin uses PoW.
ROI: Return on Investment. The % of how much money has been made comparable to an initial investment
Stablecoin: a cryptocurrency with low volatility that is pegged to a fiat currency or commodity (such as the US dollar or gold) ~ ex of stablecoins: Tether, Gemini Dollar (GUSD), DAI
Short: a margin trade that profits if the price decreases
SHA256: “secure hash algorithm” ~ the core hash technology used in blockchain to preserve the integrity amount of data
Smart Contract: self-executing digital contract using software program to automatically perform functions without the need for a third party middleman
Wallets: where you store your digital tokens (cryptocurrencies) ~ every wallet has a public key (like a username) and a private key (password).
Whale: someone that owns an incredibly large amount of cryptocurrency
Whitepaper: a document prepared for an ICO with the vision for the cryptocurrency’s use (the design, technical information, and a roadmap to explain how it will grow)
